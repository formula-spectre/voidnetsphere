World War 102, or originally known as World War Armageddon, was (is? idk) a setting that I made up to play with some friends.
it started out as a way to have an excuse to have WW2 minis and warhammer 40k/AoS minis battling each other, then it devolved into a satirical parody of 40k 9Th edition, and then it slowly mutated in an homebrew rule system based on warhammer 7Th edition, konflikt '47, OPR and homebrew rules. The setting mutated with the game, at first it was the bare minimum to justify, then I added some more lore until it became what is it now.
we originally called it Word War Armageddon of super death because it was meant as a dumb brain-emptying game, but slowly we started to shorten it in World War Armageddon.
due the nature of the "birth" of the game the setting is satirical (and grimdank too), and has many quirks.

about the setting 

the last known date is 1938; The Germans Army just enacted the Operation Valkyrie.
they tried to carpet bomb the location where Hitler was,  but something went wrong; he was badly injured, but not dead; yet this bombing started a domino effect.
no one knows exactly what lead to what, but something, deep beneath the oceans, fractured opened.
every single clock stopped. something was corrupting the very tissue of reality;
suddenly, ghosts from the past appeared; world war 1 trenches, fighting Austrian soldiers;
the first world war overlapped on the second, while it was still going.
no one understood at first what was going on, just that they had to shoot the enemy.
reality distorted; the USA and USSR were now fake allies; each one had a railgun loaded with 50 nuclear heads pointed to each other, locked in a stalemate. the cold war was overlapping too with the two world wars.

the rules

basically the rules are a mutation of various ruleset that already exists, just mix n mashed togheter and later refined to suit out concept of fun. some are very straight forward, just to get the skirmish done, some are ridicously complex to have a laugh.

Basic rules

Infantry

the infantry has the following stats:
  • Health: how many hit points before it dies
  • Armor: a protection that gives maluses when attacked
  • Ranged Weapon Skill: how good that unit is at using shooty shooty
  • Meelee Weapon Skill: How good that unit is in cqc
  • Leadership: this number determines how hard is to shock/scare/fatigue the unit
  • Movement: how many cm/inches/squares/whatever it can move
  • Actions: How many actions that unit can do in one turn
some units have other stats, but it usually depends from unit to unit.

as you can see it's quite basic; you have your unit, and you have a weapon.
to shoot with the rifle you roll off a d6 for Ranged Skill; if you score more than the number, you can shoot
let's break down the propeties of a weapon:
  • Weapon: just the weapon name
  • Type: The weapon type; every type has a positive and negative
  • range: same for movement; basically the range in which an enemy must be to target it with that weapon
  • Damage: how many wounds it deals per shot
  • Bullets: the max bullet that weapon can have loaded; you just put a dice beside the model, and each time you shoot, you reorient the dice properly
  • Penetration: how much is the weapon effective against armors
  • ablities: this can be either some keywords or just an ability specific to the weapon itself.
